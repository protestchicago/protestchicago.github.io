    Why We Protest

          We believe that people protest to address many different issues. The main goal of protesting is to make a change and have a voice. There are many different issues that people protest against, which consists of:politics, societal injustices, racial discrimination, buisnesses, wages, and more.

    Why We Care

          A large population of youth people are oblivious to injustice in their own communities. For others who are aware, there is not an effective, real-time way for a large number of people to get advance notice in time to participate. Young people need a mechanism for becoming informed about social movements, gatherings, and educational activities in their area. In short, we want more people involved in important societal issues.

         This summer we participated in the Girls Who Code Summer Immersion Program in which we were exposed to over four different programming languages during a seven week period. While we were not able to physically participate in the protests that we wanted to due to scheduling conflicts, we decided we could still contribute to the movements using the skills we learned this summer. Girls Who Code gave us a foundation to transform our idea into a working website that can impact entire communities. We hope that this website can raise awareness and connect people to stand up to make a change.
